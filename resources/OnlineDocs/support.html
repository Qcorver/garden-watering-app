Frequently Asked Questions

How does the watering advice work?

When to Water uses the Hargreaves evapotranspiration method to calculate how much water your soil loses each day. It combines this with local rainfall data and weather forecasts to determine the best day to water your garden.

Do I need any hardware or sensors?

No. When to Water works entirely with weather data. Just share your location or search for your city, and the app does the rest.

How do I enable push notifications?

When you first open the app, you will be asked to allow notifications. If you declined, you can enable them later in your device settings under Notifications > When to Water.

Does the app track my personal data?

No. When to Water uses anonymous authentication. We don't collect your name, email, or any other personal information. Read our Privacy Policy for full details.

Can I log when I've watered my garden?

Yes. Tap any day on the calendar to mark it as a watering day. This information is used to improve future recommendations, so the app knows what you've already done.

The app shows the wrong location. How do I fix this?

You can search for a different city using the search function in the app. Make sure location permissions are enabled if you want the app to detect your location automatically.

Is the app free?

Yes, When to Water is completely free to use.
